About Pakistan

In 1947, Pakistan was considered separate from India. This event in history is referred to as the partition of Pakistan, and it caused a clear separation along religious lines. Muslims fled to Pakistan, while Sikhs and Hindus went to India. After a brief period as a monarchy, Pakistan became a republic in 1956 the new Dominion of Pakistan became independent and Muhammad Ali Jinnah was sworn in as its first governor general in Karachi. Independence was marked with widespread celebration, but the atmosphere remained heated given the communal riots prevalent during independence in 1947. .

Quote of Quaid-e-Azam

"Mufakkir-e-Pakistan"

Allama Iqbal is a national hero who played a vital role in making of a separate state for the Muslims of the Sub-continent. He worked very hard with Quaid-e-Azam and no doubt, saved us from lifetime of slavery.We commemorate Iqbal Day to pay tribute to his endless efforts that has resulted in our bright future. Sir Muhammad Iqbal was a South Asian Islamic philosopher, poet and politician. His poetry is considered to be among the greatest of the 20th century, and his vision of a cultural and political ideal for the Muslims of British-ruled India is widely regarded as having animated the impulse for the Pakistan Movement.
